What is an Environmental Justice Population?

An environmental justice population in Massachusetts is a neighborhood where one or more of the following criterias are true. The first one is that annual median household income is 65 percent or less of the statewide annual median household income. Two, minorities make up 40 percent or more of the population. Three, 25 percent or more of households identify as speaking English less than “very well”. Four, minorities make up 25 percent or more of the population and the annual median household income of the municipality in which the neighborhood is located does not exceed 150 percent of the statewide annual median household income.

Environmental justice population areas in Massachusetts are identified by the Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs (EEA) using data from the 2019 American Community Survey.
