MAPC Zoning Timeline

1920

Racially Restrictive Covenants (1920s-1968)

Racially restrictive covenants were contractual agreements that prohibited the purchase, lease, or occupation of property by a specific racial group, particularly targeting Black Americans. These covenants created lasting patterns of segregation.

1934

The Federal Housing Administration's Redlining Practices (1934-1968)

Redlining by the Federal Housing Administration involved denying financial services to people based on their race or ethnicity, deeply affecting the demographic and economic landscape of neighborhoods.

1968

Suburban Zoning Policies (1968-Present)

Suburban zoning policies since the late 1960s have often exacerbated racial and socioeconomic segregation. These policies have included measures like large lot requirements and limitations on multifamily dwellings, which tend to exclude lower-income families and minorities.
